Hello there! My name is Peter Silverstein. I'm a social scientist, consumer behavior geek, and policy nerd with a passion for sustainability and climate solutions. I'm working to use an interdisciplinary approach within social science to craft climate solutions that address areas with potential for high impact and widespread adoption. I graduated from Western Washington University in 2021 with a BA in Marketing and a desire to use the consumer behavior principles I'd learned in school for sustainability aims. After two years honing my quantitative market research skills in a for-profit company, I decided to turn towards public policy to expand my horizons and improve my approach to sustainable social research.

I am currently pursuing a dual degree in Quantitative Methods for the Social Sciences and Public Administration at Columbia University. My goals within these programs are to enhance my data science skills with modern, cutting-edge tools and methodological approaches that allow me to pursue ambitious climate solutions, as well as gaining climate-focused policy analysis skills and background to make me an effective real-world contributor.

In addition to professional research experience, I am an active participant in academic research. Along with Dr. Catherine Armstrong Soule at Western Washington University and Dr. Sara Hanson at the University of Richmond, I have research in progress concerning the adoption of secondhand resale by major retail brands (e.g., Patagonia, REI, Madewell), a phenomenon we term "Secondbrand Resale." This piece of conceptual research is the first examination of this phenomenon under a consumer behavior lens and represents steps towards circularity motivated by consumer demand as well as a business case for corporate sustainable behavior. We have presented this research in various forms at the 2022 North American Conference of the Association for Consumer Research, 2022 Annual Society for Consumer Psychology Conference, and the 2024 Behavioral Insights into Business for Social Good Conference. It is currently under review at the Journal of Retailing.

Personally, I am an avid user of public transportation, buyer of used clothes and books, walker of longish and shortish walks, and baker of English muffins. Although I am a registered Democrat, I have a small collection of antique Republican president memorabilia.
